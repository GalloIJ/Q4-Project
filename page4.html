He was born in Paco, Manila, and studied at the Liceo de Manila Art School under renowned artist Fabian de la Rosa. Amorsolo later continued his studies at the University of the Philippines School of Fine Arts.

Amorsolo's works often depicted scenes of everyday life in the Philippines, particularly in rural areas. His paintings commonly featured landscapes, portraits, and genre scenes, showcasing the beauty of the countryside and the resilience of the Filipino people.

Throughout his career, Amorsolo received numerous accolades and awards for his contributions to Philippine art. In 1972, he was posthumously honored as the first National Artist of the Philippines for Visual Arts. His legacy continues to inspire generations of Filipino artists and art enthusiasts.

Notable Paintings

Here are some of Fernando Amorsolo's notable paintings:

  • Morning Meal of the Workers: This painting depicts a group of Filipino workers having their morning meal amidst lush greenery, showcasing Amorsolo's skill in capturing the essence of rural life and the resilience of the Filipino people.

  • Lavanderas: Lavanderas portrays Filipino women washing clothes by a river, highlighting the everyday tasks and chores performed by rural women in the Philippines. Amorsolo's use of light and color in this painting brings life to the scene, evoking a sense of tranquility and simplicity.

  • Palay Maiden: Palay Maiden features a young Filipina amidst a field of ripening rice stalks, symbolizing fertility and abundance. Amorsolo's portrayal of the maiden amidst the golden grains reflects the beauty of the Philippine countryside and the agricultural richness of the land.
